She Plants Marigolds

Good mourning, Sun.

Today is born.

So, cry and be free of yesterday’s grief.

From what I see, the light you lovingly breathe livens all things.

So, look at all of your creations. Love the Eden you have built.

Don’t fear the bees who love the garden and only love the Monarchs who do not sting.
They wanna sing and –also– animate all your beautiful things.

Your flowers dance with the breeze of summer’s 80’s beats, just like in their jovial spring from when your garden sprung,
and they’ll dance until their autumn comes.

Today for me, it’s barely dawn. But I feel the rise of Summer’s son.
I feel grace coursing through my roots.

I see because, thankfully, my nature promulgates truth.

A perfect view of me is incomplete without you.


Yet, perfection is a duel that may never be won.
Still, I’ll follow your lead until my winter’s begun.

Remember and never forget.

I am a Marigold, and you are my Sun.
